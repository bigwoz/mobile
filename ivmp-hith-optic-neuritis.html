Drug Overview
Indication, dose & dilution
▼
Indication
Acute optic neuritis — accelerates visual recovery
Drug
Methylprednisolone sodium succinate (Solu-Medrol)
Dose
1 gram IV daily
Course
3 days (Day 1 hospital · Days 2–3 HITH)
Diluent
NaCl 0.9% 100 mL
Standard rate
Over 30–60 minutes (100–200 mL/hr)
Cardiac risk
Over 2 hours (50 mL/hr) — arrhythmia risk
Timing
18–24 hours between infusions
Optic Neuritis ContextHigh-dose IVMP accelerates the rate of visual recovery in acute optic neuritis but does not improve final visual outcome. It also reduces the 2-year risk of a second MS event.

Contraindications
Do not administer if any present
▼
Known hypersensitivity to methylprednisolone or corticosteroids
Active systemic fungal infection
Active untreated tuberculosis
Live vaccine administered within past 4 weeks
Active uncontrolled systemic infection⚠ Discuss with prescriber before proceeding
Uncontrolled diabetes mellitus⚠ Enhanced BGL monitoring — RMO to direct frequency
Known cardiac arrhythmia risk⚠ Infuse over 2 hours only
Use With CautionPeptic ulcer disease · Osteoporosis · Glaucoma / ocular hypertension · Psychiatric history · Immunosuppression

Drug Preparation
Reconstitution · labelling · priming
▼
Solu-Medrol Act-O-Vial SystemTwo-compartment vial — diluent (top chamber) separated from powder (bottom). Activation merges them.
1
Hand hygiene
Wash or apply alcohol gel. Don non-sterile gloves.
2
Inspect the vial
Check expiry date, vial integrity. Confirm not previously activated. Remove outer protective plastic cap.
3
Activate Act-O-Vial
Press firmly down on the plastic activator button on the top to push the diluent from the upper chamber into the lower powder chamber.
4
Mix gently
Rotate and invert vial gently to dissolve powder. Do not shake vigorously.
5
Inspect the solution
Should appear clear to pale yellow. Do not use if cloudy, discoloured, or contains particles.
6
Transfer to infusion bag
Using aseptic technique, withdraw reconstituted solution and inject into NaCl 0.9% 100 mL bag. Mix gently.
7
Label the bag
Drug name · Dose (1g) · Diluent · Volume · Date & time prepared · Expiry (use within 12h) · Preparer initials.
8
Prime IV set
Spike bag aseptically, prime line fully, expel all air. Confirm no air bubbles in drip chamber or tubing.
9
Dispose of sharps
Place needles and spike directly into portable sharps container.

Adverse Effects
Recognition, severity & action
▼
STOP INFUSION · CALL 000
Anaphylaxis / Severe Hypersensitivity
Urticaria · angioedema · bronchospasm · hypotension · loss of consciousness · widespread rash
→ STOP infusion immediately · Call 000 · Lay flat, legs elevated · Activate anaphylaxis kit · Adrenaline 500 mcg IM if trained
STOP INFUSION · CALL HMO
Cardiac Arrhythmia
Palpitations · irregular or rapid pulse · chest pain · pre-syncope or syncope
→ STOP infusion · Call HITH HMO (9929 8788) · Consider 000 if haemodynamically compromised
STOP INFUSION · CALL HMO
Severe Hypertension
SBP >180 mmHg and/or DBP >110 mmHg · severe headache · visual changes
→ STOP infusion · Contact HITH medical team for urgent direction
MONITOR · CALL HMO
Hyperglycaemia
BGL >15 mmol/L · polyuria · thirst · fatigue · nausea
→ Contact HITH medical team for insulin orders or direction before continuing infusion
MONITOR · CALL HMO IF WORSENING
Neuropsychiatric Effects
Agitation · confusion · mood disturbance · insomnia · rarely — psychosis
→ Document. Contact HITH medical team if new-onset or worsening. Reassure patient — insomnia is expected.
MONITOR · CALL HMO
Hypokalaemia
Muscle weakness · cramps · palpitations (check K⁺ result if concerned)
→ Review U&E result. Contact HITH medical team if symptomatic or K⁺ <3.0 mmol/L
EXPECTED — REASSURE
Common / Benign Side Effects
Metallic taste during infusion · facial flushing · mild headache · mild mood elevation · indigestion · insomnia
→ Reassure patient — these are expected and self-limiting. Document. Advise patient to eat before/with dose if indigestion occurs.
