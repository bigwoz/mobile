Step 3 Preparation & pump setup

Preparing the furosemide bag

Furosemide 500 mg in 100 mL 0.9% sodium chloride. Skip this if pharmacy has pre-prepared the bag.

1 Remove 50 mL of fluid from the 100 mL 0.9% sodium chloride bag.
2 Draw up the total contents of 2 vials of furosemide 250 mg/25 mL ampoules via a 21-gauge (or finer) needle.
3 Add the furosemide to the 100 mL bag through the same needle.
4 Remove ALL air from the bag — ideally with the same needle, before withdrawing it, to minimise how many times the bag is pierced.
5 Label the bag: Furosemide 500 mg in 100 mL 0.9% NaCl, date/time prepared. Expiry: 24 hours from preparation.
Compatibility 0.9% sodium chloride only. Furosemide is incompatible with most medication solutions — run via a dedicated line. Check the Australian Injectable Drugs Handbook or Pharmacy for any compatibility queries.

REM Bodyguard pump — steps

1 Turn on the REM Bodyguard pump.
2 Select Continuous Program and confirm mL/hr is selected. Press START/OK.
3 Enter the bag volume (90 mL). Press START/OK.
4 Enter the dose rate for the prescribed mg/hr (see dose selector above). Press START/OK.
5 Confirm the displayed settings match the prescription before starting the infusion.
Dosing limits Standard range: 5–40 mg/hr. Start at 10 mg/hr unless altered by the HITH Consultant. Doses of 40 mg/hr or above require pre-approval from the HITH Medical Consultant. Maximum dose: 60 mg/hr (this bag will only last ~7 hours — plan for an earlier bag change).

Potassium pack contents

Carried in every HITH car, left at the patient's home awaiting instructions:

  • 2 × sachets of 5 potassium chloride slow-release (SR) 600 mg tablets, OR
  • 1 × bottle of Chlorvescent® tablets

Based on medical recommendations, the visiting HITH nurse gives the patient the required number of SR sachets and/or Chlorvescent® tablets.

PRN replacement PRN potassium replacement is charted on the medication chart per sliding scale.

Step 7 Quick reference

Indications
  • Adults aged 18 years or older.
  • Worsening heart failure symptoms with fluid overload.
  • Inadequate diuresis despite maximal intermittent IV furosemide (> 80–120 mg BD bolus doses).
  • Referrals from ED, inpatient, outpatient and community services; suitable for 8–12 hr daytime and/or 24 hr continuous infusion.
Contraindications
  • Allergy / hypersensitivity to furosemide or sulphonamides.
  • Severe hypokalaemia / hyponatraemia.
  • Severe hypovolaemia.
  • Severe hypotension (SBP < 90 mmHg).
  • Renal impairment: eGFR < 15–20 mL/min.
Medication interactions
MedicationInteraction
Aminoglycosides (e.g. gentamicin, amikacin, tobramycin)Additive nephrotoxicity with loop diuretics. If unavoidable, closely monitor renal function, auditory function and serum aminoglycoside levels.
Potassium-lowering medications (e.g. hydrochlorothiazide, high-dose laxatives)Increased hypokalaemia risk — monitor potassium.
Nephrotoxic medications (e.g. NSAIDs, ACE inhibitors, digoxin)Monitor closely if renal function significantly impaired; dosing intervals may need to be prolonged.
LithiumLoop-diuretic-induced hyponatraemia may reduce lithium clearance, increasing toxicity risk. Monitor levels and signs of toxicity.
Dosage summary
  • Standard continuous infusion range: 5–40 mg/hr.
  • Commence at 10 mg/hr unless altered by the HITH Consultant.
  • Doses ≥ 40 mg/hr require pre-approval from the HITH Medical Consultant.
  • Maximum dose: 60 mg/hr.
  • Aim for 0.5–2 kg fluid weight loss per day; adjust dose to clinical response.
