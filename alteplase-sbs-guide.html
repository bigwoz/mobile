Alteplase Preparation
Before drawing up
Adult dose (standard)2 mg in 2 mL (i.e. 1 mg/mL reconstituted solution) per lumen. Check local drug chart and policy.
Paediatric dose110% of catheter lumen volume (max 2 mg). Check patient weight and catheter priming volume.
  • 1
    Verify prescription — dose, patient identity, expiryRight patient · Right drug · Right dose · Right route · Right time
  • 2
    Reconstitute alteplase per manufacturer instructions if not pre-prepared pharmacy supply
  • 3
    Draw up into 10 mL luer lock syringe using blunt drawing-up needle. Label immediately.
  • 4
    Draw up 10 mL sodium chloride 0.9% into separate syringe/s (for post-alteplase flush)

Steps 13–17 · Partial Occlusion
Direct injection technique
  • 13
    Disinfect needleless connector (NC) — Key-Part
    Using non-touch technique: vigorous friction with 2% CHG / 70% IPA swab for ≥15 seconds. Allow to fully air dry. Do not blot, fan or blow dry.
  • 14
    Using non-touch technique — connect syringe with alteplase and slowly administer into affected lumen.

    Complete with correct clamp–disconnection sequence:
    NC TypeClamp sequence
    NegativeClamp BEFORE finishing flush & disconnecting
    PositiveClamp AFTER finishing flush & disconnecting
    NeutralNo specific clamping required
    Anti-refluxNo specific clamping required
    ⛔ STOPIf excessive resistance is evident — do not force. Stop and reassess.
  • 15
    Repeat step 14 for all affected lumens
  • 16

Step 21 · Attach Tap to CVAD Hub
Hub cleaning before disconnecting NC
Before disconnecting NCClean the catheter-NC junction, then disinfect the hub. Both require full air-dry — do not rush.
a
Using non-touch technique, hold catheter with unfolded CHG/IPA swab (non-dominant hand). Vigorously scrub connection of catheter and NC with second swab for 30 seconds
b
Allow to fully air dry
c
Ensure clamp is closed (non-valved catheter)
d
Remove NC
e
Disinfect catheter hub (Key-Part) with a new unfolded CHG/IPA swab for 30 seconds
f
Allow to fully air dry — do not blot, fan or blow dry
g
Using non-touch technique, remove cap (Micro Critical Aseptic Field) on 3-way tap and attach to catheter hub / TIVAD extension tubing (Key-Part)
Step 22 · Create Negative Pressure
Unclamp CVAD lumen. Open 3-way tap. Aspirate the empty syringe back to 8–9 mL — hold position to create a vacuum/negative pressure in the catheter lumen.
📋
Step 22 · Instructions
  • a
    Unclamp CVAD lumen (if clamp present) or unclamp TIVAD non-coring needle extension tubing
  • b
    Open 3-way tap — ensuring it is open to the empty syringe and the patient (catheter)
  • c
    Aspirate empty syringe back to 8–9 mL
  • d
    Hold the syringe in this position — this creates a negative pressure (vacuum) in the catheter lumen
Keep holdingDo not release the empty syringe — maintain the vacuum while proceeding to Step 23.
Step 23 · Open Alteplase to Catheter
While still holding the empty syringe, turn the 3-way tap off to the empty syringe and open to the alteplase syringe and patient.

Step 23 · Instructions

Step 28–29 · Failed at 120 Minutes
Second dose or extended dwell decision
Occlusion not cleared after 120 minutesConsider the following options — consult team as appropriate:
  • a
    Second alteplase dose — repeat steps 22–24 with a fresh alteplase dose. Assess again after a further 120 minutes (Step 29).
  • b
    Extended dwell — consider extending dwell time to 24–72 hours (requires medical review)
  • c
    Low-dose continuous infusion of thrombolytic medication (requires medical order and specialist input)
EscalateDiscuss with vascular access expert, nurse practitioner / CNC, and medical team before proceeding.

Remove 3-way tap · replace NC · flush
Blood successfully withdrawnWithdraw 5 mL blood and discard before flushing.

Step 31 · Remove 3-Way Tap

Using non-touch technique: hold catheter with unfolded CHG/IPA swab, vigorously scrub catheter hub/tap junction with another swab for 30 seconds
Allow to fully air dry
Ensure clamp is closed (non-valved catheter)
Remove 3-way tap set-up and discard
Using non-touch technique: attach new needleless connector (NC) — Key-Part

Step 32 · Pulsatile Flush — NaCl 0.9%

Adults: 20 mL using 2 × 10 mL syringes or 1 × 20 mL syringe — pulsatile (push–pause) technique
Paediatrics: Clinically appropriate — at minimum double the CVAD volume or as per adult doses
Flush using pulsatile (push–pause) technique
Complete with correct clamp–disconnection technique for NC type: Negative NC → clamp BEFORE finishing flush / Positive NC → clamp AFTER / Neutral & Anti-reflux → no specific sequence

Step 33 · Continue / Lock

Commence prescribed IV fluids or medications, OR lock with 20 mL NaCl 0.9% or locking solution as per local policy
