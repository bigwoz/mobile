📋
Before you start
Applies to CICC, tc-CICC, PICC, TIVAD (portacath) & apheresis-CICC
  • Assess and document dressing condition at least once per shift, and at every outpatient, clinic or home visit
  • Replace dressing every 7 days, or earlier if it contains ooze/moisture or is not intact
  • Ensure IV lines are secured to the patient to prevent pulling on the dressing
Prompt intervention — do not wait until the next dressing change is due.

Standard care checklist
Skin antisepsis
  • 2% CHG in 70% IPA swab sticks (e.g. Chloraprep®, 3M™ Soluprep™, Reynard Foam Swab Stick®)
  • Prolonged antisepsis as per local policy — CHG disc (e.g. Biopatch®) or CHG gel dressing (3M™ Tegaderm™ CHG)
Securement & dressing
  • Use engineered securement devices (ESD) — adhesive or subcutaneous
  • Sterile TSM, ideally next-generation bordered dressing
  • Apply alcohol-free skin barrier film under dressing materials if any risk factors present
Critical practice pointsAllow skin to completely air dry before applying any dressing materials. Use low-and-slow, controlled technique for dressing removal while supporting the skin.
📌
Patient education
  • Look at the dressing every day; notify the healthcare team of discomfort, burning, itching, or moisture under the dressing
  • Encourage hydration and a balanced diet for skin health
  • Clip hair under the dressing if required to assist adherence
